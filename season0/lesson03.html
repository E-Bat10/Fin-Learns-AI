Mini‑lesson: How to verify

  • Prefer .gov, .edu, reputable .org, textbooks, or peer‑reviewed sources.
  • Check dates. Out‑of‑date info can mislead.
  • Triangulate 2–3 independent sources; avoid confirmation bias.

Claims to test today:

  • “Lightning never strikes the same place twice.” — False, it can strike repeatedly.
  • “North Carolina’s capital is Charlotte.” — False (it’s Raleigh).
  • “Ray tracing increases FPS.” — Usually false; it’s heavier on the GPU.
  • “Sea turtles are reptiles.” — True.
